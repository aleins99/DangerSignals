FLAMMABLE

Flammable materials are those which ignite more easily than other materials, whereas those which are less easily ignited or which burn less vigorously are combustible.

The degree of flammability or combustibility depends lagerly upon the chemical composition of the subject material, as well as the radio of mass versus surfaces area.

RADIOACTIVE

The process by which an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emiting radiation, such as an alpha particle, beta particle with neutrino or only a neutrino in the case of electron capture, gamma ray, or electron in the case of internal conversion. A material containing such unstable nuclei is considered radioactive.

BIOHAZARD

Sustances that pose a threat to the health of living organisms, primarily that of humans. This include nothing or samples of a microgarnism, virus or toxin (from a biological source) that can affect human health. It can also include sustances harmful to the other animals.
